Some Breast Cancer Statistics

World Wide Statistics

In 2020, about 2.3 million women were diagnosed with breast cancer worldwide and 685,000 died. Every 14 seconds , somewhere in the world, a woman is diagnosed with breast cancer. It is the most common cancer in women. In 2012, it represented about 12 percent of all new cancer cases and 25 percent of all cancers in women.

United States Statistics

In 2023, an estimated 297,790 women will be diagnosed with breast cancer in the U.S., making it the most common cancer in American women. Every two minutes a woman in the U.S. is diagnosed with the disease.From 2005 to 2014, the most recent 10 years for which data are available, it showed that it is more common in black women.

Survival Statistics

The five- and 10-year relative survival rates for women with invasive breast cancer are 91 percent and 84 percent, respectively. There are more than 4 million breast cancer survivors in the United States, including women still being treated and those who have completed treatment.
